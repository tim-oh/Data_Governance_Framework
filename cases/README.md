# Country cases

This space presents four country case studies illustrating how different national contexts have approached data governance challenges in digital social protection systems. The cases are drawn from Uganda, the Philippines, Türkiye, and Chile.

The cases are illustrative rather than evaluative. They should not be read as endorsements of particular approaches or as evidence of general effectiveness.

## Cases

| Country | Theme | Key pillars |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| [Uganda](uganda.md) | Expanding social protection | Management, Quality |
| [Philippines](philippines.md) | The road to interoperability | Access, Management |
| [Türkiye](turkiye.md) | Approach to dynamic data | Quality, Management |
| [Chile](chile.md) | 'Only ask once' policy | Access, Quality |

## How to read the cases

Each case follows a consistent structure: country context, data governance challenge, approach taken, outcomes and lessons, and a mapping to the framework's pillars and mechanisms.

Use the cases to identify approaches that may be relevant to your context, and follow the links to the relevant building block and toolkit pages for implementation guidance.
